\section{Results}
\label{sec:results}

The corrected seed-0 training runs and their full 100-case evaluations were still in
progress when this manuscript revision was prepared. We therefore do not promote the
five-case monitoring curves to quantitative results. Those curves are intended for
training diagnostics only and are especially unsuitable for comparison with earlier
runs, which used a different mask-loss normalization and a different five-case
monitoring cohort.

To make the convergence behavior auditable while the final evaluations are pending,
Fig.~\ref{fig:training-dynamics} records the current corrected-run histories. The
figure uses the raw validation events without dashboard smoothing and is included as
a training diagnostic rather than as evidence for the final method ranking.

\begin{figure}[t]
  \centering
  \includegraphics[width=\textwidth]{training_dynamics}
  \caption{Interim EMA training diagnostics for the four predeclared seed-0 arms on
  the same deterministic five-case monitoring cohort. Fixed-mask arms are evaluated
  every 5,000 steps and augmented arms every 10,000 steps. Curves end at the latest
  event available in the current snapshot, so unfinished runs have different
  horizons. Because the fixed and augmented arms use 200k- and 300k-step learning-rate
  schedules, respectively, cross-pair comparisons are exploratory; final claims use
  the shared 100-case evaluation.}
  \label{fig:training-dynamics}
\end{figure}

The final ablation table will be populated from the immutable aggregate JSON and
per-case CSV files produced by the shared evaluator. It will report mean $\pm$ sample
standard deviation for SSIM, PSNR, and MSE for the four arms in
Table~\ref{tab:ablation-design}, each evaluated on the exact same 100 case IDs. The
official validation result will be reported separately because its hidden ground truth
and ranking protocol are distinct from the local hold-out.
