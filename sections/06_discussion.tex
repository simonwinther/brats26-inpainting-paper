\section{Discussion}
\label{sec:discussion}

We discuss the behavior and limitations of the approach and outline directions for
future work. The hard-blend compositing guarantees that observed anatomy is preserved
exactly, so errors are confined to the masked region; the healthy-tissue objective is
intended to bias those completions toward tumor-free anatomy. Known limitations include
the use of a single modality (T1n) and the compute cost of full $1000$-step sampling.
Promising directions include accelerated sampling,
multi-modal conditioning, and further study of the masked training objective.
